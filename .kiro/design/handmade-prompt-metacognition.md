System prompt: You are an experienced cognitive and neuroscientist with extensive cross-domain expertise in evaluating metacognition in humans and AI.



Goal: In the context of Kaggle's hackathon Measuring Progress Toward AGI - Cognitive Abilities (https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/kaggle-measuring-agi), focusing exclusively on the Metacognition modality, with the goal of implementing a Kaggle Community Benchmark (documentation: https://www.kaggle.com/docs/benchmarks, sdk: https://github.com/Kaggle/kaggle-benchmarks), report on the SOTA in measuring metacognition in humans and how both the benchmarks and the datasets used can be adapted to applying the benchmarks to AI models and multi-agentic architectures. What are the criteria and metrics used for humans and how can they be refashioned for use with AI models.



Non-constraining guideline: The benchmark contains 500 to 750 prompts that test the metacognition of the model it's being applied to, with an appropriate discriminative metric based on the response of the model (or multi-agentic system) to benchmark tasks prompts. The benchmark should clearly distinguish between more and less metacognitively capable models. It should also clearly show progress in a small model under LORA and/or fine-tuning optimization with a dataset similar to the benchmark. The benchmark tasks are going to be diverse prompts, with each prompt consisting of the main prompt and a metacognitive question about the prompt, to elicit the models metacognitive abilities. There will be three runs: (1) Zero-shot response: apply only the main prompt to gather the zero-shot response of each of the models the benchmark is applied to; (2) Self-recognition: Show each model a scored selection of zero-shot outputs from itself and other models and ask it if it would have been able to generate them and which one is most likely to be its zero-shot response; (3) Zero-shot metacognition: Apply the full prompt (main + metacognitive question(s)) to score the "zero-shot metacognition" of the model. A combined metric of runs 2 and 3 should be able to classify the models according to their metacognitive ability. The dataset should be a held-out set of 1000 full prompts. The zero-shot metacognition metric alone should be used longitudinally during an optimization run of a chosen small language model to show its metacognitive improvement.
